The constitution of the people’s party:

The agenda of the people’s party is democracy.

As a political class within a republic we represent

the interest of our constituents directly.

The positioning of people’s party representatives

in public office, and direct voting from constituents,

 catalyzes true democracy within the republic.

This constitution provides the framework for

two actions by representatives.

Voting :

People’s party representatives vote

in accordance to the people’s* vote.

Proposing Legislation :

To be brought to the floor legislation

must be petitioned by a majority of

a representative’s constituents.

Notice* : Representatives of the people’s party

must identify themselves by one of three sub parties.

Constituental :

When voting Constituental representatives

consider their constituents the people.

Majoritorial:

When voting Majoritorial representatives

consider all people of the republic the people.
Independent:

When voting Independent representatives

may take their respective Constituental

opinion or the Majoritorial alternative.
