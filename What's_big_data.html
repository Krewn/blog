What's big data?:
        As far as I'm concerned the first two aspects an analytical solution are the size and update frequency or velocity of the underlying data. Next we consider the data attributes and our dashboard components or graphs. With a minimal viable prototype in place consider availability and deployment. The end goal is to give users a high quality window into a data set, quick access to the slices of data that contain actionable intelligence provides efficiency.

'National Institute of Standards and Technology. NIST argues that big data is data which “exceed(s) the capacity or capability of current or conventional methods and systems.” '  and from the same article, ' Gartner. In 2001, a Meta (now Gartner) report noted the increasing size of data, the increasing rate at which it is produced and the increasing range of formats and representations employed. This report predated the term “dig data” but proposed a three-fold definition encompassing the “three Vs”: Volume, Velocity and Variety.This idea has since become popular and sometimes includes a fourth V: veracity, to cover questions of trust and uncertainty.'

While considering these three Vs of data let's look at GoogleSheets, and it’s limits. (We will ignore veracity for today). Users view data, modify it in real time and create insightful plots and graphs. While most users do not hit the limitations of this system they do exist.

'mjg76 said:

Sharing refers to explicitly assigning access permissions to the document or spreadsheet.  You can allow 200 people access to the document or spreadsheet, but only 50 people can be simultaneously editing the document at any given time.  For presentations, only 10 people can edit at the same time. '

This means that the maximum availability of the data is 200 clients and the maximum velocity is 50 clients * the maximum input rate per client. Next we can examine the analytical limitations of the platform. (Let me just say google sheets is awesome and no-one here is complaining.)

  • Number of Formulas: 40,000 cells containing formulas
  • Number of Tabs: 200 sheets per workbook
  • GoogleFinance formulas: 1,000 GoogleFinance formulas
  • GoogleLookup formulas: 1,000 GoogleLookup formulas
  • ImportRange formulas: 50 cross-workbook reference formulas
  • ImportData, ImportHtml, ImportFeed, or ImportXml formulas: 50 functions for external data 

Now we see a fuller picture of what sheets is as a tool and what features it provides for live data streams and large computational tasks. Considering all this, and knowing the popularity of GoogleDrive and its core office features, we might consider these limitations as the boundary of big data. With that said we can recognize that a user doing analysis on sheets is not doing big data while the developers of google Sheets are because the data set containing your sheets is actually far larger than anything you could open in any standard office product.

Alas we have to consider another aspect of data. While the world of spread sheets is warm and comfortable for most programmers data comes in all shapes and sizes, and by that I mean files extensions. For example while data for a human genome may containgigabytes of dotty sequencing images the underlying genome inferred my processing these images contains only 20 - 30 thousand genes, and could be stored in a google doc!

So in the world of bioinformatics using tools (probably not google sheets) to browse genomes is not big data, while the work to actually inference a genome from a sequence of images is.

What is this transformation of big data into little data? The answer to this question is the job of data scientists. The ability to create availability of quality information.
