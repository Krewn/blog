Chapter 1:

It seems to me that the idea that we could understand understanding is paradoxical.

Certainly we could correlate measurable neural activity to “engrams” as individuals  partake in exercises; doing math, recalling a sequence of images or faces, using a computer etc… but to fundamentally understand the integrity of an individual experience still escapes us. The scientific explanation for conscious has not yet been simply and indisputably stated.

Throughout life most people experience some level of spirituality. Atheists, agnostics, theists, and radicals alike necessarily take on a foundational set of premises to build an existential rationality. Unfortunately today the power of thought is restricted. Encumbered by dogma the proliferation of sentience seems stale.


        Trapped behind our own technology in terms of time and responsibility we isolate ourselves through the use of extremities such as weapons and exchanges to pursue personal gains. Indoctrinating children through public education while revoking their ability to develop professional skills, depending on shadow games to live fruitful lives while small farmers and “off the grid folk” become exceedingly sparse and massive monocrop expenses fill the remaining green space. Our planet's ecosystem is changing at unprecedented rates and over a decade has passed since Al Gore made that exceedingly obvious. Yet with the capacity to make real changes economic “realities” have created a century old controlling debt, which has undeniably infiltrated the integrity of our government considering the relationship between the federal reserve and the department of defense considering the lack of any audit.

        With the internet we should be making world peace not microtransactions. We need to take responsibility for our planet and yet the primary goal of people has become to take legal responsibility of a livable space, clean water and electricity for themselves and beyond that the cost of education drives a life dominated by the contribution to profitable professional endeavours. Usury of property both intellectual and material property results in pain and opportunity cost. Diseases and even causes of death such as starvation go unaddressed for economic reasons. The “separation of church and state” disarms most people's moral tactility when addressing legal matters. Meanwhile our military budget  has become a majority expense.

I think the only appropriate premise to accept for now is that something exists.
