On company:

        Earth is our vessel. Finite in every way its expanse has become exhausted by claims. These claims, when recognized, comprised our departure from nature. A new age emerged and with it a new reason to murder. Humanity wars when and where efforts to dominate exist. Assets make for targets in the mind of desperate individuals. Over time Interests aggregated: the deposition of ethical boundaries emerged as relativism took hold. Outside of war moral considerations became entangled by legalities.

        The question of what is lost when nationality is gained is central here, that is, what of our obligation to accommodate dissent? I believe that individuals’ equality is not satisfied when law favors one morality over another. Moreover, when the law applies geographic considerations in company with restrictions to persons movement we must capitulate it’s designed to sustain social stratification.

        Aristotle believed that anything that exists is a product of nature or craft, never both. So what of inequality?  Historically it has been proposed that social strata are a product of nature, and in some cases it is. Sexually dimorphic characteristics and speciation are just a few natural distinctions. In modern human culture, though, the haves and have nots are not determined by physical realities but rather social ones. The Nazi’s, slavers, crusaders and spartans  alike believed that they were superior by some grace of nature, exaggerating the moral implications of extraneous attributes such as; skin hair and eye color, as well as religion. In the company of likeminded individuals governors crafted laws not to oppress people. Such a thing would be reprehensible. Rather, laws were created in accordance with a particular perception, and like any good self fulfilling prophecy people subjugated by these laws were made to be less than those empowered by them. The fundamental reality herein is that while a state provides a cohort of similar companions it naturally stratifies people by revoking the personifying permanence of citizenship.

So what of today? Certainly situations should always strive for improvement. Yet in the context moral relativism how is the individual valued against society? The local context of family and the mutualism of comradery invoke connectedness between us, and presently currency represents debts. The abstract shift presented by this material representation presents an inexorable catalyst for extortion. Furthermore dynamic evaluation of means and ends provides a multiplier for subjective capital gains against minimal tangible contribution. Naturally in complement to this pitfall the economy has yielded a landslide of production and technical innovation.
