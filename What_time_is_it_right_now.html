What time is it right now?

        Seriously I don’t want to talk your ear off about railroad time, I will deduce here a morality from the consequences of a few basic principle arguments. I don’t mean to keep you long but if you have a minute I would really appreciate you seeing this through. The conclusion is simple really, and I hope you can draw this out for yourself. From a canvas in landscape draw a horizontal line with an arrowhead on one end (congratulations you just labled your dominate hand).

In all seriousness though this arrow represents you! Pointing along your perception of time the arrow head is you right now. Exciting right? Right here right now you have the opportunity to live a miraculous life. As I’m writing this now, you may or may not be alive, and as you read this I may have also passed. Which begs the question, What time is it?

I would be vain to think that anyone will really be musing over my words after I pass, but I would be remiss not to speak as if they were. That is to say: taking into consideration every person who could ever read this there is no way that I could answer this question correctly with an answer like 1:54pm and yet I can tell you what time it is. Now I know there are a number of you out there who stopped reading when I said that I would tell you what time it is. Some of you are thinking, “well, if he gives a number between 0 and 13 the odds aren’t too bad”, and perhaps a few others are counting up minutes in a day, but that’s honestly beyond the point and if we could all just keep reading we could get around to answer to the original question. That is now. Right now.

Consciousness exists explicitly between the past and future. No matter when you read this the same is true between us. That reality is sacred, and it is the first fundamental piece to understanding kindness. Love will be our case study here. With the predicate of our consciousness on the razor's edge between the past and future, cogito ergo sum (I think therefor We must recognize that each individual has different means, and the similar needs. There is no exception to mammal's need for sustenance and social behavior, and yet the variety of mammalian kindness is almost as ubiquitous. From a Gerbil in sandy plains heroically drawing attention to itself sounding the alarm of terrestrial predators, to a kid who has a pet gerbil there are instances of kindness between individuals everywhere.


        So that line, you. Let’s draw another line me. Some time before now I wrote this down. Now you are reading this. The path that this idea took from my mind to yours is in the direction of time and across our two conscious. The distance between consciousness is the height of our landscape. This idea is a vector with the direction  < myNowΔyourNow , MeΔYou > it has a temporal component written as myNowΔyourNow the difference between myNow (the time I’m writing this) and yourNow (the time you are reading this) as well as a special pseudo spatial component that is the difference between my conscious realization of this idea and yours.

Now if you have been so faithful and drawn the idea from me to you, with time in mind (and perhaps some trigonometry) you have a diagonal. This diagonal line is the idea and it is comprised of some difference in time and space. It is interesting to think of one’s self as a point on the horizontal; the point your arrow draws to is you, one of infinite perspectives at a given moment.

It is that, consideration of the moment beyond self along the vertical. The thin space in which our consciousness exists. The device of empathy. Love exists in this space, between two people in space together at the same time. This consideration of coincidence is beyond any force of newtonian nature. Inexplicably capable, the mind is ironically a hard box to think outside of.
