Complexity and productivity:

So let's start with this; if we cannot relate to a plot or graph, it doesn't matter how much information it contains, it might as well be the matrix. Consequently actionable intelligence becomes general intelligence, as the true meaning of the information we digest materializes as a relationship to the data we can hold on to.

So why does this matter? Let's think about some real data for a moment. We look at plots of stock prices over time and perhaps we feel the vicarious excitement or terror of being a holder at different peaks and troughs. In this way we understand the data because we are able to abstract some points on an emotional dimension and construct an imaginative response from there.

In this spirit here are some plots of gross on return ratio and investment. Effectively green is viability red/ yellow is profitability and blue represents a considerable loss of capital.

Here we have 0% through 200% return ratio plotted, while in reality the opportunities that that regularly appear are most likely (assuming that they are at-least some what viable) recoverable for some where between 80% and 110%. In which case the plot looks more like this.

    Notice that the orange along the bottom of the graph represents 0 gross, as opposed to green in the first graph. Also notice that our 0 or break even point has moved up the color bar, from green in the first plot to orange, reflecting the fact that there is more to be lost here than there is to be gained. Underlining the importance for intelligent decision making.

So let's think a little more about this. I was networking where it came up that once you can get returns even one percent above 100, all you need is traffic. For me the concept is a powerful reality and standard by which viabilities should be evaluated.

Ethics aside the implications of these small percent profits as percentage of expenditure should be understood by everybody in the business space.

        The accumulation of streamline efficiency is an incredible force. It can propel small ventures explosively and over time it can erode almost any entity. So in the spirit of such incremental iteration lets take one more look at our plot of gross on investment and return ratio between 99 and 100 percent.
