Now this is many existentialists' favorite topic, and I want to take some time to talk about it here.

First let's address the argument that the pace of video games improvement indicates a trajectory that will bring our simulated experiences up to the human experience. The fundamental problem here is that this argument fails to address the existence of conscious experience. As a programmer and entrepreneur Elon know's that the value of a product is derived from use, for example Google wouldn't predict what you were going to search if no-one had ever searched on Google. Furthermore, it is well known to programmers that results of computations are predetermined by machine state at the time of execution. Even random number generation is procedural and a random bit is actually predictable given enough knowledge of the machine when you ask for a random number. Many articles like this one exist to publish algorithms for random number generation and this strict compliance to a set of instructions means there is no thought contained by a program. Thought is provided by the user, and where things are predetermined (as in a program) thought is not required, and as the universe takes the path of least resistance, where thought is not required thought does not exist.

The progress of our computers has been a process of refining and scaling a small number of fundamental implementations spanning large periods between the development of new base technologies. Considering how few fundamental processing paradigms we have known in the history of computing it seems unlikely to me that humanity will conceive of a conscious computer before the conclusion of the species.

Human, Mechanical, Analog, Digital, Quantum.

Computers have existed in effectively 5 forms in human history. First the original definition, a human computer or person who does math. Historically this process was tedious as compared to our modern capacity but highly valuable none the less.  Second we have had mechanical computers as early as Egypt where a string with 11 evenly spaced knots was used to make a 3-4-5 right triangle. More recently we have had digital computers which utilize electrical states and properties of large components such as transistors, capacitors, and tapes. We also have powerful analog computing capacity making use of quantum effects such as diffraction and black body effects to create data sources for more traditional digital computers. For example the implementation of photo sensitive transistors used by Digital Single-Lens Reflex (DSLR) cameras to create digital images is an example of parallel analog computing. Similar to digital computers analog computers do not make use on mono-atomic components. This final category of computers, quantum computers, interrogate and manipulate the electro-magnetic environment around single atom (phosphorus) in a suspension of a neutral host (silicon). With all these developments in computers they have never been able to think, rather they compute.

The distinction between thinking and computing may not be immediately apparent to everyone so I want to put it in as few words as possible, and that is this, computation is deterministic, where though is whimsical. To elaborate, knowing the state of a computer at a given time, we can calculate its state at any arbitrary point in the future (neglecting component failure for simplicity). Once you incorporate a conscious entity such a prediction cannot be made. Let's look at a word processor for example. In a vacuum the only thing that changes is the cursor blinking. Add a user to the situation though, and it is not possible to make an exact prediction to the future state of the machine.

And really that's the rub. Without considering the counter argument I will provide shortly, it is conceivable to make such predictions about human behavior given enough information about the state of the person in high enough resolution. The same way we can calculate the rate of chemical processes or turbulent effects in dynamic systems couldn't we model a person? And by all means we could try. We can make robots that walk, talk, interpret tactile sensation, see, hear, smell and even evolve. What we cannot do tho is make a computer that thinks.

If I'm a simulation there is no reason for me to be conscious, and I cannot speak for you but I am conscious.

We can make a robot that to an outside observer appears a conscious being, but as its makers we know the underlying logic, and we know our programs have about as much consciousness as a line of dominoes. To synthesize a true consciousness from raw material and knowledge would be to look God square in the eye. We just aren't there, and while articles like this one attempt to get us closer it almost seems like too much.

Finally addressing the the choice "Either we will create a simulation that is indistinguishable from reality, or, civilization will cease to exist." I see a third option, and I believe that Elon does too, and that is a civilization that is not dependent on a single sun. A space faring intelligence who can leverage the true scale of time and, perhaps, unlock the deepest secrets of the universe.
